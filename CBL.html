Columbia Build Lab connects MBA founders with an idea to talented Columbia undergraduate and graduate students who can help create and scale that idea into a minimum viable product (MVP).

About CBL

Launch a successful startuptart and bring your talents to develop a minimum viable product (MVP).

Entrepreneurship

Columbia Build Lab (CBL) targets the earliest stage of entrepreneurial development, providing grounds for MBA students at Columbia Business School to transform their ideas into products/services.

Talents

CBL plays the fundamental role of a matchmaker, connecting MBA founders with the valuable undergraduate/graduate talents at Columbia University needed to make their ambitions reality.

Why CBL?

Experience

At CBL, founders and students will be able to gain first-hand experiences by kickstarting a project and working in startups with great potentials. It will provide great opportunities for students to gain insights into other industries.

Networking

CBL houses startups that are founded by Columbia Business School students. Therefore, CBL provides opportunities for MBA students, undergraduate and graudate students in various fields to make new connections.

Academic Credit

Every semester that you work with CBL, undergraduate students can gain up to 3 academic credits. For Computer Science students, it can be counted towards an elective course.
