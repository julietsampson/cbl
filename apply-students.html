1

Apply and Interview

Applications are on a rolling-basis. Make sure to use the application that is specific to the role you are applying for. The interview will be scheduled with a board member from CBL within two weeks. For software engineering roles, there will be a short coding challenge. For other roles, be prepared to talk about your experiences/projects.

2

Decisions released

After the interview, we will send you lists of startups. You will then rank them based on your preferences.

3

Get connected

CBL will match you to the startups based on your preferences. Although first choice is not guaranteed, we will try our best to match you to the startups based on your rankings.

4

Work over the semester

Once notified about your startup match, we will introduce you to the founders, and you will be able to start your internship.

5

End-of-Semester Presentation

For students who have applied for academic credits, they will be required to give a short end-of-semester presentation of works they have done to the advising professor.

Benefits of CBl

Experience

At CBL, you will be able to gain first-hand experiences in startups with great potentials. It will not only count as an internship experience, but it will also be a great opportunity for you to gain insights into other industries.

Network

CBL houses startups that are founded by Columbia Business School students. You will be able to make new connections with MBA students and other undergraduate/graduate students in various fields.

Academic Credit

Every semester that you work with CBL, you will be able to gain up to 3 academic credits. For Computer Science students, it counts as an elective course (Projects in Computer Science - COMS W4901).

Fellowship

Engineering / tech undergraduates:

You are eligible to receive a $1800 fellowship for your work during your second semester with CBL after successful completion of one cohort. One semester equals to an average of 10 hours per week, for 12 weeks.

Engineering graduate students:

You are eligible to receive a $3000 fellowship upon acceptance to CBL. One semester equals to an average of 10 hours per week, for 12 weeks.
